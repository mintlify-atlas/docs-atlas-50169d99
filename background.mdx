## Educational Journey

I'm currently studying **Multiplatform Application Development** at **IES Oretania**, a vocational training program that's shaping my future as a developer. This intensive course focuses on building applications that work seamlessly across different platforms and devices.

<Note>
  Multiplatform Application Development (DAM - Desarrollo de Aplicaciones Multiplataforma) is a higher vocational training cycle in Spain that prepares students to create applications for mobile, desktop, and web environments.
</Note>

### Why Multiplatform Development?

I chose this path because I'm fascinated by the challenge of creating solutions that reach users regardless of their device or platform. In today's connected world, applications need to work everywhere - from smartphones to tablets, from Windows to macOS, from Android to iOS.

## Roots in Linares, Jaén

I'm from **Linares**, a historic city in the province of Jaén, in the autonomous community of Andalusia, southern Spain. Linares has a rich industrial heritage and a vibrant community that has shaped who I am today.

### About Linares

<Tip>
  Linares is located in the northern part of Jaén province, known for its mining history, beautiful architecture, and as the birthplace of legendary guitarist Andrés Segovia.
</Tip>

Growing up in Linares has given me:
- A strong sense of community and collaboration
- Appreciation for both tradition and innovation
- The determination to pursue opportunities in technology, even from smaller cities
- Connection to Andalusian culture and values

### Studying at IES Oretania

**IES Oretania** is my educational home, where I'm gaining both theoretical knowledge and practical skills in application development. The institute provides:

- Hands-on training with real-world development scenarios
- Modern programming languages and frameworks
- Project-based learning approaches
- Preparation for professional certification

<Note>
  The curriculum covers everything from mobile app development with Android and iOS, to desktop applications, databases, web services, and deployment strategies.
</Note>
